## 2. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site function, remember your preferences, and understand how the site is used. Similar technologies, such as pixels, tags, and local storage, work in comparable ways, and this policy covers them too.

---

## 3. Types of Cookies We Use

### 3.1 Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to work. They enable core features such as page navigation, security, and access to protected areas. Without them, parts of the site would not function properly. Because they are essential, they cannot be switched off through our consent tools.

### 3.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting aggregated information about pages visited, time spent, and general behaviour. We use this to improve the site and our services. Analytics data is used in a way that does not identify you personally wherever possible.

### 3.3 Marketing Cookies

These cookies may be used to measure the effectiveness of our campaigns and, where applicable, to show relevant content or advertising. They may track your activity across sessions. We only place marketing cookies where you have given consent.

### 3.4 Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by third-party services we use, such as analytics platforms, embedded content, and communication tools. These providers set their own cookies and process data under their own privacy policies. We do not control cookies set by third parties, and we encourage you to review their policies.

## 4. Consent

When you first visit our website, we present a cookie banner that lets you accept or manage cookies. Necessary cookies are always active because the site cannot function without them. Analytics and marketing cookies are only placed after you give consent, where consent is required by law.

You can change or withdraw your consent at any time through the cookie settings on our website.

---

## 5. Managing Cookies

You have several ways to control cookies:

- Use the cookie settings or preferences tool on our website to accept, reject, or adjust non-essential cookies.
- Adjust your browser settings to block or delete cookies. Most browsers let you refuse cookies or alert you when a cookie is being set.
- Use browser privacy modes or extensions that limit tracking.

Please note that blocking some cookies may affect how the website works and may limit certain features.

---

## 6. Browser Controls

Each browser handles cookie management differently. You can usually find these controls in the settings or preferences menu under privacy or security. Common browsers include Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge, and each provides instructions in its help documentation for viewing, blocking, and deleting cookies.
